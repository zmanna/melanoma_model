# Commercialization Readiness

## Bottom Line

DermaScan has prototype value, but it is not commercially ready. It should be treated as a seed asset for diligence, not a product ready to sell.

The correct commercialization posture is disciplined optimism: the repo proves that a team can build an end-to-end demo, but the real business value depends on data rights, clinical validation, workflow design, regulatory strategy, trust, and distribution.

## Readiness Scorecard

| Dimension | Current Readiness | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prototype functionality | Medium | Upload-to-prediction path exists. |
| Product experience | Low | UI is basic and not designed around clinical workflow. |
| Model performance evidence | Low | Metrics exist, but reproducibility and validation are incomplete. |
| Dataset governance | Low | Exact dataset subset and license details need documentation. |
| Security/privacy | Low | Uploads are local and not protected. |
| Regulatory posture | Low | No medical-device strategy documented. |
| Clinical workflow | Low | No clinician-in-the-loop design. |
| Commercial narrative | Medium | Strong as a portfolio and diligence case study. |

## Best Near-Term Market Position

The safest near-term positioning is not autonomous diagnosis. Better possibilities:

- educational dermatology AI demo
- internal model-risk case study
- clinician-facing research prototype
- portfolio project showing AI product governance
- technical foundation for a future regulated decision-support workflow

## What Would Need To Be True

For this to become a serious product candidate:

1. Data rights must be clear.
2. Training and evaluation must be reproducible.
3. Performance must be externally validated.
4. The product must avoid autonomous diagnosis claims.
5. Security and privacy controls must be rebuilt.
6. Regulatory counsel must classify the intended use.
7. Clinical advisors must shape the workflow.
8. The team must define who acts on model output and how.

## Capitalization Strategy

The commercial opportunity should be pursued in stages:

| Stage | Goal |
|---|---|
| Asset cleanup | Make the repo credible, navigable, and safe to inspect. |
| Technical rebuild | Reproduce training and establish evaluation discipline. |
| Product discovery | Identify the workflow where AI support creates value. |
| Clinical validation | Test performance with appropriate external review. |
| Compliance planning | Determine regulatory, privacy, and security requirements. |
| Pilot design | Build a limited, supervised, non-diagnostic pilot if justified. |

## Claims To Avoid

Do not claim:

- clinically validated melanoma detection
- diagnostic accuracy
- patient safety
- HIPAA compliance
- FDA readiness
- production readiness
- replacement for dermatologists

## Claims That Are Supportable

With the current repository, it is fair to claim:

- end-to-end educational prototype
- Flask and TensorFlow integration
- CNN-based binary image classifier
- model tracking and documentation artifacts
- strong awareness of medical AI risks
- clear plan for professionalizing the asset
